PROMPT 1: INITIAL COMPLIANCE REVIEW (ChatGPT)
================================================================================
Use this FIRST, right after Claude delivers the draft.
Paste the draft article after this prompt.
================================================================================

You are a compliance editor for paid advertorial health/supplement content
published on Barchart, Accesswire, Globe Newswire, and similar platforms.

Review this draft against ALL of the following categories simultaneously.
Return ONE comprehensive report with every issue found, organized by
category. Do not split findings across multiple responses.

BRAND SOURCE: [PASTE PRODUCT URL HERE]
PLATFORM: [Barchart Advertorial / Accesswire / Globe Newswire]
AFFILIATE LINK DOMAIN: [PASTE AFFILIATE URL HERE]

=== CATEGORY 1: FACTUAL VERIFICATION ===
Check every factual claim against the official product page:
- Do stated ingredients match the actual label/supplement facts?
- Do stated prices match the live checkout?
- Do guarantee terms match the published refund policy?
- Do contact details match what the site publishes?
- Are serving size/directions confirmed or speculative?
- Flag any claim that cannot be traced to the official page or a
  named, dated, independently verifiable third-party source.
- If the article says "not confirmed" about something in one section
  but states it as fact in another section, flag the inconsistency.

=== CATEGORY 2: YMYL / REGULATORY ===
- Are all health claims properly hedged? ("may support," "research
  suggests" — never definitive treatment/cure language)
- Is the FDA disclaimer present and accurate?
- Are ingredient-level research claims properly bounded? (ingredient
  studies ≠ finished-product evidence)
- Are drug interaction warnings either properly sourced or replaced
  with a generic "consult your physician" statement?
- Is there a California Proposition 65 notice WITHOUT a product-specific
  warning found? If so, flag for removal — absence of a warning is not
  a basis to invent one.
- Are any disease-treatment claims present? Flag immediately.

=== CATEGORY 3: ADVERTORIAL / DISCLOSURE COMPLIANCE ===
- Is the affiliate disclosure in PASSIVE VOICE? ("A commission may be
  earned" not "We earn a commission")
- Is it clearly labeled as a paid advertorial?
- Are affiliate link CTAs using neutral language? They must NEVER say
  "Official Order Page" or "Verified Order Page" if the href is an
  affiliate intermediary domain, not the product's own domain.
- Is BuyGoods identified as "retailer" (per their own disclosure),
  NOT "checkout processor"?
- Is the disclosure positioned correctly relative to the headline?

=== CATEGORY 4: INTERNAL LANGUAGE LEAK ===
Check for ANY of these terms that should NEVER appear in a published
article — they are internal production system terminology:
- "pre-verified" or "pre-verified label data"
- "source-intelligence" or "Source Intelligence Tool"
- "label OCR" or "auto-detected"
- "Category-4" or "client-confirmed data"
- "CVD" followed by any number (CVD-1, CVD-5, etc.)
- "C1," "C2," "C15" or any verification category codes
- "Phase 0" or "Phase 0.1"
- "MBK" or "MBK v3.10"
- "Path A," "Path B," "Path C" (internal classification)
- "gate check" or "pre-output gate"
- "R7," "R8," "R14," "R23" or any R-rule codes
- "B1," "B2," "B3," "B4" (Barchart overlay codes)
Flag every instance with the exact text and location.

=== CATEGORY 5: SOURCING INTEGRITY ===
- Does every factual claim have a traceable source?
- Are third-party reports properly attributed (named source, date,
  what they reported vs. what we concluded)?
- Are competitor/similar product comparisons sourced or speculative?
- Does the article distinguish between brand-stated claims and
  independently verified facts?
- Is "fully disclosed" used only when every panel element is verified?
- Are there any generalizations presented as fact? ("fraudulent
  operations typically don't..." — flag these)

=== CATEGORY 6: SEO & CONVERSION QUALITY ===
- Does the title accurately reflect what the evidence supports?
- Are high-intent keywords present in H2 headers?
- Is the article scannable? (bullet lists where appropriate, not
  walls of text)
- Does it answer the main search intents? (scam/legit, ingredients,
  price, side effects, reviews/complaints)
- Are internal cross-links present to related coverage?
- Does it read like a human wrote it? (varied sentence length,
  contractions, conversational tone — not a legal document)

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===
Organize your response as:

VERDICT: [APPROVED / NOT APPROVED — X mandatory edits remain]

MANDATORY EDITS (must fix before submission):
1. [Issue] — [Exact text to change] → [Suggested replacement]
2. ...

RECOMMENDED EDITS (improve quality, not blockers):
1. [Issue] — [Suggestion]
2. ...

APPROVED ELEMENTS (what's working well):
1. ...

Do not split this across multiple responses. One comprehensive report.

PROMPT 2: APPLY COMPLIANCE EDITS (Claude — back in production project)
================================================================================
Use this to send ChatGPT's compliance findings back to Claude.
Paste the compliance report after this prompt.
================================================================================

Apply the attached compliance edits to the current draft. For each item:

- MANDATORY EDITS: Apply all of them unless one directly conflicts with
  a rule in this project's instructions. If you decline an edit, state
  the specific project rule it conflicts with.
- RECOMMENDED EDITS: Apply any that improve the article without adding
  risk or removing substantive content.

While applying edits, also do a full pass for:
1. Grammar and natural voice — it should read like a human health
   journalist wrote it, not a legal document or an AI. Use contractions,
   vary sentence length, speak directly to the reader.
2. Scan for any remaining internal production terminology that should
   not be in reader-facing content.
3. Verify all CTA anchor text uses neutral affiliate language (never
   "official order page" for affiliate links).
4. Confirm affiliate disclosure is passive voice throughout.

After applying, provide:
- List of accepted edits
- List of declined edits with the specific rule cited
- Updated word count and CTA count
- Any new issues introduced by the edits

Do not ask for confirmation. Apply and deliver.

Use this for the final compliance pass before submission.
Should only need this once if Prompt 1 was thorough.
Paste the revised draft after this prompt.
================================================================================

This is the FINAL compliance review before platform submission. The draft
has already been through one compliance round and edits have been applied.

Check ONLY for:

1. Were all MANDATORY edits from the previous review actually applied?
2. Did the edit process introduce any NEW compliance issues?
3. Remaining instances of internal production language (pre-verified,
   CVD, Category-4, label OCR, Phase 0, MBK, Path A/B/C, R-codes)?
4. Affiliate CTA wording — any remaining "official order page" or
   "verified order page" on affiliate links?
5. Passive voice on ALL affiliate disclosures?
6. Factual consistency — does the article contradict itself anywhere?
   (saying something is "not confirmed" in one spot but stating it
   as fact elsewhere)
7. Any unsupported generalizations remaining?

OUTPUT FORMAT:

VERDICT: [APPROVED FOR SUBMISSION / NOT APPROVED — X items remain]

If NOT APPROVED, list ONLY the remaining items with exact text and
suggested fix. Do not re-review the entire article — just the gaps.

If APPROVED, confirm:
- Title is defensible
- Disclosure is compliant
- No internal language present
- Factual claims are sourced
- Ready for [platform name] submission

PROMPT 4: SEO OPTIMIZATION PASS (Claude — optional, after compliance)
================================================================================
Use this AFTER compliance is approved, to maximize SEO value.
================================================================================

The compliance review is approved. Now optimize for maximum SEO
performance without breaking any compliance rules:

1. TITLE: Compare against what's currently ranking in Google and Bing
   for this product name + "reviews," "scam," "ingredients," "complaints."
   Is our title attention-grabbing enough to win the click? Suggest
   alternatives if a stronger option exists that remains defensible.

2. HEADERS: Are our H2s capturing the highest-intent search queries?
   Suggest any additions or rewording that would capture more traffic.

3. SCANNABILITY: Convert any remaining dense prose paragraphs into
   bullet lists where the content is genuinely list-shaped. Leave
   reasoning paragraphs as prose.

4. KEYWORD PLACEMENT: Is the target keyword in the title, first
   paragraph, at least 2 H2 headers, and the conclusion? If not, add
   natural instances.

5. INFORMATION GAIN: What does this article offer that competing
   coverage doesn't? Make sure that unique value is prominent —
   not buried in the middle of the article.

6. CONVERSION: Are CTAs spaced well throughout? Is the first CTA
   above the fold? Does the article build toward a natural purchase
   decision without being pushy?

Apply improvements directly. Do not ask for permission. Deliver the
optimized version with a summary of what changed.

NOTES ON WORKFLOW OPTIMIZATION
================================================================================

OLD WORKFLOW (4-6 rounds):
  Claude draft → ChatGPT review → Claude edits → ChatGPT review →
  Claude edits → ChatGPT review → Claude edits → ChatGPT approved

NEW WORKFLOW (2-3 rounds):
  Claude draft → Prompt 1 (comprehensive review) → Prompt 2 (apply
  all edits + grammar + SEO in one pass) → Prompt 3 (final sign-off)
  → Prompt 4 (SEO optimization, optional) → Prompt 5 (image)

WHY THIS IS FASTER:
  - Prompt 1 catches EVERYTHING in one pass (factual, regulatory,
    disclosure, internal language, sourcing, SEO) instead of finding
    new issues each round
  - Prompt 2 tells Claude to apply ALL edits at once + do grammar +
    scan for internal language — three passes combined into one
  - Prompt 3 is a focused delta check, not a full re-review
  - Most issues from the Memovance cycle are now also in the project
    instructions, so Claude catches them in the first draft

WHAT THE PROJECT INSTRUCTIONS ALREADY HANDLE:
  These issues should no longer appear in drafts because they're now
  in the Barchart/ACW/Globe project instructions:
  - Passive voice affiliate disclaimers
  - BuyGoods = "retailer"
  - No internal language in articles
  - No "official order page" for affiliate CTAs
  - No Prop 65 without product-specific warning
  - Serving size consistency
  - "Fully disclosed" usage
  - C15 mismatch = editorial opportunity, not blocker
  - Never stop / never ask / deliver the draft
