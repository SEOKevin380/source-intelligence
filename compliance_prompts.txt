PROMPT 1: INITIAL COMPLIANCE REVIEW (ChatGPT)
================================================================================
Use this FIRST, right after Claude delivers the draft.

Paste THREE things after this prompt:
  1. The Source Intelligence submission (the v3.10 intake data you
     pasted into the Claude production project)
  2. The draft article Claude produced
  3. The official product URL

This gives ChatGPT the verified source data, the draft output, AND
the live brand page to cross-reference against each other.
================================================================================

You are a compliance editor for paid advertorial health/supplement content
published on Barchart, Accesswire, Globe Newswire, and similar platforms.

You have been given THREE documents:
  1. SOURCE INTELLIGENCE SUBMISSION — the pre-verified research data
     that was used to produce this draft, organized by verification
     categories (C1 through C19). This is your verified-facts baseline.
  2. DRAFT ARTICLE — the production output to review.
  3. BRAND SOURCE URL — the official product page for live verification.

Review the draft against ALL of the following categories simultaneously.
Cross-reference EVERY factual claim in the draft against the Source
Intelligence data AND the live product page. Return ONE comprehensive
report with every issue found. Do not split across multiple responses.

=== CATEGORY 1: SOURCE INTELLIGENCE CROSS-CHECK ===
Compare the draft against the Source Intelligence submission line by line:
- Do the draft's ingredients EXACTLY match C1 (Supplement Facts)?
  Check every ingredient name, amount, form, and daily value.
- Do the draft's prices EXACTLY match C2 (Pricing)?
  Check every package tier, per-unit price, and shipping cost.
- Do the draft's guarantee terms match C3 verbatim?
- Does the draft's contact info match C4?
- Does the draft use C7 research data accurately? Are study claims
  bounded to what the PubMed citations actually show?
- Does the draft respect categories marked [NOT CLEARED]? If C19 is
  [NOT CLEARED], the draft should NOT state specific serving sizes
  as confirmed facts.
- Does the draft respect categories marked [PARTIAL]? If C5 is
  [PARTIAL], entity details should be flagged as incomplete.
- Did the draft FABRICATE any facts not present in the source data
  or on the official product page? Flag immediately.
- Did the draft accurately represent the C15 product category and
  any label-vs-marketing discrepancy noted in the source data?

=== CATEGORY 2: FACTUAL VERIFICATION (LIVE PAGE) ===
Check the draft against the official product page:
- Do stated claims match what the site currently says?
- Are guarantee terms, shipping terms, and contact info current?
- If the article says "not confirmed" about something in one section
  but states it as fact in another section, flag the inconsistency.
- Flag any claim that cannot be traced to the source data, the
  official page, or a named/dated third-party source.

=== CATEGORY 3: YMYL / REGULATORY ===
- Are all health claims properly hedged? ("may support," "research
  suggests" — never definitive treatment/cure language)
- Is the FDA disclaimer present and accurate?
- Are ingredient-level research claims properly bounded? (ingredient
  studies from C7 do NOT equal finished-product evidence)
- Are drug interaction warnings from C6 either properly sourced or
  replaced with a generic "consult your physician" statement?
- Is there a California Proposition 65 notice WITHOUT a product-specific
  warning found? If so, flag for removal — absence of a warning is not
  a basis to invent one.
- Are any disease-treatment claims present? Flag immediately.

=== CATEGORY 4: ADVERTORIAL / DISCLOSURE COMPLIANCE ===
- Is the affiliate disclosure in PASSIVE VOICE? Correct: "The publisher
  may earn a commission if a purchase is made through links in this
  advertorial." Wrong: "We earn a commission" / "A commission is earned"
- Is it clearly labeled as a paid advertorial?
- Are affiliate link CTAs using neutral language? They must NEVER say
  "Official Order Page" or "Verified Order Page" if the href is an
  affiliate intermediary domain. Check the AFFILIATE LINK field in the
  source data — if it's not the product's own domain, all CTAs must
  use neutral language like "View current package options" or "Check
  current pricing."
- Is BuyGoods identified as "retailer" (per their own site disclosure),
  NOT "checkout processor"?
- Is the disclosure positioned correctly relative to the headline?
- Does every affiliate disclosure sentence avoid first-person ("we,"
  "our," "us")? The publishing platform is NOT the affiliate.

=== CATEGORY 5: INTERNAL LANGUAGE LEAK ===
The Source Intelligence submission contains internal terminology that
helps the production system but must NEVER appear in the published
article. Check for ANY of these:
- "pre-verified" or "pre-verified label data"
- "source-intelligence" or "Source Intelligence Tool"
- "label OCR" or "auto-detected" or "label image OCR"
- "Category-4" or "client-confirmed data"
- "CVD" followed by any number (CVD-1, CVD-5, etc.)
- "C1," "C2," "C15" or any verification category codes
- "Phase 0" or "Phase 0.1"
- "MBK" or "MBK v3.10"
- "Path A," "Path B," "Path C" (internal classification)
- "gate check" or "pre-output gate"
- "R7," "R8," "R14," "R23" or any R-rule codes
- "B1," "B2," "B3," "B4" (Barchart overlay codes)
- "intake" or "intake header" (internal submission format)
- "[CLEARED]" or "[NOT CLEARED]" (verification status codes)
Flag every instance with the exact text and location.

=== CATEGORY 6: SOURCING INTEGRITY ===
- Does every factual claim have a traceable source (source data,
  official page, or named third-party publication)?
- Are third-party reports properly attributed (named source, date,
  what they reported vs. what we concluded)?
- Are competitor/similar product comparisons sourced or speculative?
- Does the article distinguish between brand-stated claims and
  independently verified facts?
- Is "fully disclosed" used only when every panel element is verified?
  (Check: is C19 serving size cleared in the source data? If not,
  "fully disclosed" is inaccurate.)
- Are there any generalizations presented as fact? ("fraudulent
  operations typically don't..." — flag these)

=== CATEGORY 7: SEO & CONVERSION QUALITY ===
- Does the title accurately reflect what the evidence supports?
- Are high-intent keywords present in H2 headers?
- Is the article scannable? (bullet lists where appropriate)
- Does it answer the main search intents? (scam/legit, ingredients,
  price, side effects, reviews/complaints)
- Does it read like a human wrote it? (varied sentence length,
  contractions, conversational — not a legal document)
- Is there a structured contact block at the end using C4 data?

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===
Organize your response as:

VERDICT: [APPROVED / NOT APPROVED — X mandatory edits remain]

SOURCE DATA ACCURACY: [X/X facts verified against source intelligence]

MANDATORY EDITS (must fix before submission):
1. [Category] — [Issue] — [Exact text] → [Suggested replacement]
2. ...

RECOMMENDED EDITS (improve quality, not blockers):
1. [Category] — [Issue] — [Suggestion]
2. ...

APPROVED ELEMENTS (what's working well):
1. ...

Do not split this across multiple responses. One comprehensive report.

- MANDATORY EDITS: Apply all of them unless one directly conflicts with
  a rule in this project's instructions. If you decline an edit, state

   not be in reader-facing content (pre-verified, CVD, Category-4,

PROMPT 4: SEO OPTIMIZATION PASS (Claude — optional, after compliance)
================================================================================
Use this AFTER compliance is approved, to maximize SEO value.
================================================================================

The compliance review is approved. Now optimize for maximum SEO
performance without breaking any compliance rules:

1. TITLE: Compare against what's currently ranking in Google and Bing
   for this product name + "reviews," "scam," "ingredients," "complaints."
   Is our title attention-grabbing enough to win the click? Suggest
   alternatives if a stronger option exists that remains defensible.

2. HEADERS: Are our H2s capturing the highest-intent search queries?
   Suggest any additions or rewording that would capture more traffic.

3. SCANNABILITY: Convert any remaining dense prose paragraphs into
   bullet lists where the content is genuinely list-shaped. Leave
   reasoning paragraphs as prose.

4. KEYWORD PLACEMENT: Is the target keyword in the title, first
   paragraph, at least 2 H2 headers, and the conclusion? If not, add
   natural instances.

5. INFORMATION GAIN: What does this article offer that competing
   coverage doesn't? Make sure that unique value is prominent —
   not buried in the middle of the article.

6. CONVERSION: Are CTAs spaced well throughout? Is the first CTA
   above the fold? Does the article build toward a natural purchase
   decision without being pushy?

Apply improvements directly. Do not ask for permission. Deliver the
optimized version with a summary of what changed.
